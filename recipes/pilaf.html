Pilaf

Description

Pilaf, a dish made from meat, rice, onions, carrots and spices, is included in the UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage List.

Ingredients

  • Mutton - 1 kg
  • Mutton fat - 200 g
  • Carrot - 1 kg
  • Onions - 3-4 pieces
  • Vegetable oil - 50 ml
  • Garlic - 1 head and 1 clove
  • Iranian cumin - 2 teaspoons
  • Barberry - 2 teaspoons
  • Red pepper - 1 teaspoon
  • Salt - to taste
  • Rice - 1 kg

Cooking steps

  1. Cut the meat and lard into medium pieces, the carrots into strips, and the onion into half rings.
  2. Throw the lard into the pan and render the fat over medium heat.
  3. Remove the cracklings and add oil. Fry the peeled garlic clove until golden brown, then remove it.
  4. Pour half of the fat and oil into the cauldron. And in what is left in the frying pan, fry the carrots until golden brown.
  5. Place the cauldron on medium heat, add the onion with a pinch of cumin and brown.
  6. Increase the heat, add the meat and fry until golden brown, stirring constantly.
  7. Mix the meat with carrots and add water so that its level is one and a half centimeters higher than the contents of the cauldron.
  8. Add barberry, cumin, red pepper and salt. Cook for about 60 minutes on low heat.
  9. After an hour, place an unpeeled head of garlic on the meat and cover it with rice in an even layer. First, rinse the rice several times.
  10. Add water so that its level is one and a half centimeters above the rice.
  11. Increase heat to high and cook until water evaporates.
  12. Gather the rice to form a mound in the center of the pot.
  13. Take a stick and make holes in several rows. Make sure that they reach the very bottom.
  14. After 2 minutes, remove from heat, cover and wrap in a towel soaked in cold water.
  15. Leave the pilaf for about 40 minutes.

The dish is ready! Enjoy your meal!
